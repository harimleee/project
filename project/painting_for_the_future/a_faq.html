  • Subscription application
    5 article in this Topic

  • How do I sign up?

    To sign up for a website member, click the person icon on the top right of the main screen and you'll see the sign up button.

  • How do I apply for a subscription?

    After logging in, click Join us and you can subscribe by number of months.

  • How can I register as a family or group?

    If you would like to support each family or group member under the name of each member, please apply individually through the website join us. If you are uncomfortable with the large number of members required to register, please call [206) 709-3100].

  • How do unsubscribe?

    Call the sponsored representative number [02-2078-7000] and you can process it. It may be a hassle, but please contact me when you can talk to me on the phone below.

    "Time for TelephoneConsultation"
    - 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. on weekdays.
    - Telephone counseling is not available on Sundays and holidays.

  • Why can't I check my subscription information on the home page?

    Children and business sponsorship information is registered on the website within 2~3 days of applying for sponsorship. If you do not see any details on the [Child Support] or [Project Support] menu after 2 to 3 days of application for sponsorship, please contact us at our representative phone [206) 709-3100].

  • Child support
    6 article in this Topic

  • Can I send a gift to the child?

    If you send it to Painting for the Future, it will be delivered to the sponsored child.

    Sending address : 440 5th Ave N. Seattle, WA 98109 (206) 709-3100 ext.7100

    When delivering letters/gifts, please make sure to fill in the [sponsor number / sponsor name / child number / child name] and send it to us.

  • How can I send a letter to a child?

    If you send it to Painting for the Future, it will be delivered to the sponsored child.

    Sending address : 440 5th Ave N. Seattle, WA 98109 (206) 709-3100 ext.7100

    When delivering letters/gifts, please make sure to fill in the [sponsor number / sponsor name / child number / child name] and send it to us.

  • Can I get a child's reply if I send a letter to the child?

    The reply written by the child will be received from the Korea World Vision office and sent to the sponsor along with the Korean translation written with the help of the translators.

    ※Note※
    It may be difficult to read English letters in Africa because they are cursive.

  • Can I let the child know my contact number?

    Under the 'Child Protection Policy' stipulated by the World Vision International Headquarters, Painting For the Future does not inform the sponsor of the child's contact information nor the sponsor's contact information. I ask for your understanding.


    If the content of the letter to the child is left with your contact or email address, delete this part and forward it to the child. In addition, you cannot enclose business cards in mail such as gifts/letters.


    However, when you send a letter, you can write down your sponsor's address on the envelope, and we will change your address from Painting for the Future to a new envelope without your address.

  • I'd like to know how children are doing.

    In addition to letter exchanges, the mail that your sponsor can check on children's news is as follows.

    1) Welcome Package: Within 10 days of application for sponsorship, you can receive a foreign child sponsorship guide and a children's national introduction with child photos.

    2) Child introduction letter: Within 4 months of applying for sponsorship, you can receive a letter introducing children.

    3) Child Growth Newsletter: The latest photos and recent status of children are reported once a year (Summer to Autumn).

    4) Year-end card: We send you a Christmas card or New Year's card written by children at the end of each year/year.

  • Is the child's regular subscription cash to the child?

    Sponsorships are used for 'cash support' of education, living and medical expenses, depending on the circumstances and needs of children and families, and also for 'service support' of programs (after-school learning, special education, camp participation, emotional education programs, etc.).
